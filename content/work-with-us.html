The project

The heart of the project is to understand what makes genes silent in vivo. Recent work has focused on gene activation, but surprisingly little is known about how genes are silenced in normal conditions. Our work suggests that the bulk of silencing in Drosophila does not require transcription repressors, nor repressive histone marks.

To further study this surprising observation, we are developing the TRiP (Thousands of Reporters in Parallel) technology. The essence of TRiP is to integrate a gene at thousands of different locations in a genome and study its transcription in the new context. In combination with publicly available datasets, this allows to answer the following questions:

  1. Does the sequence of a gene determine its expression in every chromatin environment?
  2. Does the local chromatin environment have a predictable effect on the expression of the gene?
  3. Does the local topology have an predictable effect on the expression of the gene?
  4. Do the same rules apply to all the genes?

For more information about this line of research in our team, checkout the paragraph about Invasion landscapes.
