E. coli heat-shock competent cells preparation

Used for heat-shock transformation.

Material:

  • E. coli strain
  • LB medium
  • 0.1 M CaCl2 solution (ice cold)
  • LB plates with proper antibiotic
  • 0.1 M CaCl2 solution containing 15% glycerol (ice cold)

Procedure:

The day before

  1. Put the 0.1 M CaCl2 solution and 0.1 M CaCl2 solution containing 15% glycerol at 4 °C.
  2. Inoculate one single colony of E. coli strain in 5 mL LB medium. Shake at 37°C overnight.

  1. Put at least thirty 1.5 mL Eppendorf tubes at -80°C.
  2. Inoculate 1 mL overnight culture in 100 mL LB medium within a 500 mL flask.
  3. Subculture at 37°C with shaking till OD600 reaches ~ 0.25-0.3 (about 2 hours subculture time).
  4. Chill the culture on ice for 15 minutes.
  5. Separate 100 mL chilled bacterial culture into two 50 mL Falcon tubes and centrifuge at 4°C at 4000 rpm for 10 minutes.
  6. Discard the supernatant and resuspend the pellet with 40 mL ice-cold 0.1 M CaCl2 solution.
  7. Keep cells on ice again for 30 minutes.
  8. Centrifuge cells at 4000 rpm at 4°C for 10 minutes.
  9. Discard the supernatant and resuspend pellet with 5 mL ice-cold 0.1 M CaCl2 solution containing 15% glycerol.
  10. Pipet 50 μL of cell suspension into -80°C frozen eppendorfs and directly transfer them to -80°C freezer.
