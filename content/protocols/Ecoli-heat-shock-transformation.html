E. coli heat-shock transformation

Material:

  • 50 μL competent cells
  • plasmid DNA
  • heating block at 42°C
  • LB plates with proper antibiotic

Procedure:

  1. Turn on the heating block at 42°C.
  2. Take competent cells from -80°C freezer and thaw them on ice.
  3. Pipette 10 ng (3-5 μL) of DNA plasmid into competent cells while competent cells are starting to melt.
  4. Keep cells with transformed DNA plasmids on ice for 5 minutes.
  5. Heat-shock cells at 42°C for 45 seconds.
  6. Keep cells on ice again for 2 minutes.
  7. Spread totally 50 μL cells with transformed DNA plasmids on a LB plate with proper antibiotic.
  8. Keep LB plates at 37°C and pick colonies after 12-16 hours incubation.
