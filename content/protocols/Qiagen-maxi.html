Modified Qiagen maxi-prep

Some parts of this protocol have been optimized for speed and ease of use. Using 100 mL culture allows to perform all the steps in Falcon tubes and a medium speed centrifugation. This is then easier to scale up the number of maxi-preps in parallel. The yield is routinely more than 200 μg for high copy number plasmid.

Material

  • 100 mL bacterial culture
  • Qiagen Plasmid Maxi kit (Cat. No. 12163)
  • Isopropanol
  • 70% Ethanol

Procedure:

When you open the kit

  1. Add RNase A and LyseBlue to buffer P1.
  2. Store buffer P1 and buffer P3 at 4°C
  3. Store buffer P2 at room temperature.

Bacteria culture

  1. Pick a single colony of E. coli from a plate and inoculate a starter culture of 5 mL LB medium containing the appropriate antibiotic.
  2. Incubate overnight at 37°C with shaking at ~ 300 rpm.
  3. Inoculate 1 mL of a starter culture into a flask with 100 mL LB medium containing the appropriate antibiotic and grow at 37°C for more tan 12 hours with with shaking at ~ 300 rpm.

  1. If a white precipitate (SDS) is visible in buffer P2, heat the bottle in an incubator or in water bath until it disappears.
  2. Seperate culture into two 50 mL Falcon tubes and centrifuge at 4000 rpm for 10 minutes.
    3. at 4°C.
  3. Resuspend the bacterial pellet with 10 mL buffer P1 containing RNase A and LyseBlue.
  4. Add 10 mL buffer P2, mix by inverting the tube until uniformly blue and leave at room temperature for 5 minutes.
  5. Add 10 mL chilled buffer P3, mix by vigorously inverting until blue color disappears, and place the tube on ice for 20 min.
  6. Centrifuge the Falcon tubes at 4000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4°C.
  7. Meanwhile, equilibrate the Qiagen column by washing with 10 mL buffer QBT.
  8. Carefully decant the liquid phase into the column. Some white debris will follow, but try to minize it.
  9. Wash the column 2 times with 30 mL buffer QC.
  10. Elute DNA with 15 mL buffer QF in a 50 mL Falcon tube.
  11. Precipitate DNA by adding 10.5 mL room-temperature isopropanol to the eluate. Mix and centrifuge at 4000 rpm for 30 minutes at 4°C.
  12. Wash DNA pellet with 5 mL of room-temperature 70% ethanol, and centrifuge at 4000 rpm for 15 minuntes.
  13. Spill the liquid. The pellet should form a visible white ring at the bottom of the tube.
  14. Dry the walls of the tube with a tissue. Do not touch the pellet.
  15. Dissolve the pellet in 100 μL of ddH2O.
