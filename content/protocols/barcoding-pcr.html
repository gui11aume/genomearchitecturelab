Barcoding PCR

Some parts of this protocol have been optimized for efficiency and yield. See the comments below for complements.

Material:

  • Phusion polymerase (New England Biolabs M0530S)
  • T4 DNA polymerase (New England Biolabs M0203S)
  • Concentrated T4 DNA ligase (New England Biolabs M0202M)
  • 500 mM EDTA
  • 5'-phosphorylated barcoded primers

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the following PCR mix.
    componentvolume (μL)
    H2O14
    5x GC buffer4
    10 mM each dNTPs0.5
    10 μM forward primer1
    10 μM reverse primer1
    5 ng/μL plasmid0.2
    Phusion polymerase0.25
  2. Run it with the following programme.
    1.98°C1 min.
    2.98°C20 sec.
    3.60°C30 sec.
    4.72°C3 min.
    5.go to step 2. 29 times
    6.72°C10 min.
    7.12°C∞
    8.75°C20 min.
  3. After the PCR reaction is finished the machine will cool down to 12 °C (PCR step 7). Add 1 μL T4 DNA polymerase to the tube.
  4. After 20 minutes add 0.5 μL 500 mM EDTA to the tube and press "next step".
  5. When the run is finished purify the DNA with a Qiaquick PCR extraction kit and resuspend in 50 μL EB.
  6. Prepare the following ligation mix.
    componentvolume (μL)
    H2O7
    10x DNA ligase buffer2
    Concentrated DNA ligase1
    PCR product10
  7. Incubate for 16 hours at 16 °C

Comments on the protocol

Barcoded primers contain stretches of random nucleotides in 5'. It is more convenient to barcode only one of the primers because the PCR has a better yield and it reduces the formation of forked products.

Forked PCR products have mismatched strands at one end. If only one primer is barcoded, half of the PCR products are forked, and if both primers are barcoded all the PCR products are forked.

The treatment with T4 DNA polymerase removes the forks by generating a double-stranded barcode. This makes the PCR product fully double-stranded, so that it can be ligated.
