Kc cells DNA extraction protocol

This protocol Kc167 cells.

Material:

  • Extraction Buffer:
    [0.8M guanidine thiocyanate, 5 mM CaCl2, 20 mM EDTA, 5% Tween 20, 0.5% Triton X-100, 50 mM HEPES, pH 5.3]
  • Proteinase K
  • RNase A
  • Chloroform
  • Iso-propanol
  • 70% ethanol

Procedure:

  1. 1.5 mL Kc cells (per well) are cultured into a 6-well plate and grown till the appropriate density for genomic DNA extraction.
  2. Transfer 1.5mL Kc cells into a 1.5mL eppendorf and centrifuge at 2000 rpm for 3 minutes.
    *Use more cells for extracting genomic DNA if cells are not condensed enough.
  3. Discard the supernatant after centrifugation. Resuspend cell pellet with 500 mL extraction buffer containing 200µg of proteinase K and 10 µg/mL RNase A.Vortex homogeneously and incubate the sample at 55℃C for 15 minutes.
  4. Add 600µl chloroform, vortex homogeneously for creating an emulsion.
  5. Centrifuge the sample at maximum speed at 4℃C for 5 minutes.
  6. Transfer the supernatant into a new 1.5mL eppendorf (avoid taking any cell debris around the surface between the supernatant and chloroform) and add 350 mL iso-propanol*. Vortex gently and centrifuge the sample at maximum speed at 4℃C for 5 minutes again. *After adding iso-propanol, generally you are able to see the while suspension precipitated.
  7. Discard the supernatant and add 70% ethanol into the eppendorf and vortex gently. Centrifuge the sample at maximum speed at 4℃C for 5 another minutes.
  8. Discard the supernatant. Do not dry DNA pellet more than 1 minute. Dissolve DNA pellet immediately in appropriate volume of distilled water.
